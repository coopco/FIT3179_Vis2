The 2019-20 Australian bushfire season

The Leadup to the Season

Last year's Australian bushfire sesaon, which lasted from September 2019 to Match 2020, resulted in the largest bushfire in recorded history.

By the end of the season, over 17 million hectares had been burned, resulting in:

  • 33 people losing their lives
  • 3094 homes destroyed
  • Losses of over 1 billion animals nationally, with over 800 million of those in New South Wales

One major contributer to the intense fires was the fact that 2019 was Australia's hottest year on record. This resulted in conditions prone to ignition and an increase in the acceleration of fire spread.

Historical Temperature Anomaly

2019 also saw the occurence of a very rare weather event called a sudden stratospheric warming (SSW).

This resulted in very dry weather throughout the year, and made 2019 Australia's driest year on record. This, combined with the hottest year on record, results in a climate highly susceptible to intense bushfires.

An SSW of this severity has only ever been recorded once in 2002, now Australia's second driest year on record.

SSWs are not believed to be caused by global warming, instead considered a natural phenomenon.

The Extent of Damage

The state of New South Wales was worst affected, with 2439 homes lost and the burned area covering a quarter of the state's entire forested area.
